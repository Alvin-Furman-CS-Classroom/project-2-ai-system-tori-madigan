Full example — 4×4 medium logic worksheet

Pet type · Movie genre · Dream vacation · Hobby

Four friends each have exactly one pet, one favorite movie genre, one dream vacation, and one main hobby. No two friends share any category value, and every value in each list is used once across the four people. Your job is to decide which hobby goes with which pet, genre, and vacation by marking the worksheet: place a check where a pair is true and an X where it is impossible.

Variables: A = pet type · B = movie genre · C = dream vacation · D = hobby.
Columns follow A, then B, then C. Rows are grouped as D (hobby), then C (vacation), then B (genre).
Each unordered pair of different variables appears in exactly one region of the sheet, so you never mark the same comparison twice in two different places.

Clues

Hints (full sentences)

  1. The friend whose hobby is reading has always wanted a beach vacation and would never pick a cruise as the dream trip.
  2. Whoever owns the rabbit lists cooking as their hobby, not reading or gaming.
  3. The dog owner spends weekends gaming and does not name sci-fi as their favorite movie genre.
  4. Exactly one friend names comedy as their favorite genre, and that same friend is the one who owns the cat.
  5. The person who trains a bird spends free time on sports, not cooking, and does not pair that hobby with a city break.
  6. Whoever loves drama films dreams of visiting a big city, and no one associates drama with a mountain retreat.
  7. The friend whose dream vacation is a cruise is also the one who prefers horror over comedy and drama.
  8. Each pet, each genre, each dream vacation, and each hobby appears exactly once in the final solution, with no repeated values in any column of the finished story.
